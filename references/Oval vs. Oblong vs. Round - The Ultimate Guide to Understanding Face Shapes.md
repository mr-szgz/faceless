## 1\. Oval Face Shape – The Ideal Everyone Talks About

**Key Measurements**

-   Face length is about 1.5 times the width
-   The forehead is slightly wider than the chin
-   Cheekbones are the widest part and sit high
-   Jawline tapers gently no sharp angles, no square corners

## How to Spot Oval Shape Face?

Just stand in front of a mirror at your home, pull your head back, and look straight on. If your face looks like an upside-down egg. It means you have an oval face shape. That’s simple, this is to find the oval shape.

## Why do People Call the Oval Face Shape Ideal?

On an oval face shape, all the styles like hairstyle, glasses shapes, and beard styles look very balanced compared to other face shapes. This is the reason people call it the most **[ideal face shape](https://whatismyfaceshape.net/determine-your-face-shape-manual-vs-ai.html)**.

-   **Hair:** You can do any haircut with an Oval Face Shape. As we mentioned earlier, all the styles look balanced on such face shapes
-   **Avoid:** Try to avoid extremely long straight hair if you do not have good hair volume.

## 2\. Oblong Face Shape

**Key Measurements**

-   Face length is noticeably longer than width (usually 1.7 1 or more)
-   Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are almost the same width
-   Sides of face are fairly straight (not much taper)
-   Chin is often slightly pointed or square

## How to Spot It?

Measure your face from the hairline to the chin. If the length is much longer than the width, you have an Oblong Face Shape. You don’t need to make it too complicated.

### Best Styling Tips for Oblong

-   **Hair (women):** Medium-length with waves or volume on the sides (think curtain bangs, shoulder-length layers, side-swept bangs). Avoid super-long straight hair with a center part.
-   **Hair (men):** Side part, textured crop, fringe forward, undercut with volume on top and tighter sides. Avoid slicked-back long hair or high skin fades that expose the forehead.
-   **Beards (men):** Grow it fuller on the sides and shorter on the chin (think friendly mutton chops or a wide-boxed beard) to add width.
-   Glasses: Wide, oversized, or strong horizontal frames (wayfarers, square, aviators). Avoid narrow or rimless frames.
-   **Makeup (women):** Horizontal contour under cheekbones, highlight sides of forehead and chin to shorten.

## 2\. Round Face Shape

**Key Measurements**

-   Length and width are almost equal (ratio close to 1:1)
-   Cheekbones are the widest part
-   Jawline and hairline are soft and rounded very little angle
-   Face looks as wide as it is tall

## How to Spot It?

Stand in front of a mirror and see if your face looks like a circle or a full moon; then it means you have a round face shape.

### Best Styling Tips for Round

-   **Hair (women):**Long layers, high ponytails, volume at the crown, side-swept or asymmetrical bangs. Avoid blunt bobs that end at the jaw.
-   **Hair (men):** Pompadour, quiff, undercut with height on top, forward fringe. Avoid center parts and bowl cuts.
-   **Beards (men):** Grow it longer on the chin (goatee, Van Dyke, or anchor beard) to lengthen. Keep sides short.
-   **Glasses:**Angular frames (rectangular, cat-eye, wayfarers). Avoid round or small frames.
-   **Makeup (women):** Contour the temples and jawline vertically, highlight the top of the cheekbones, and the center of the forehead.
